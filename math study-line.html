1. Algebra ‚Äî Variables & Equations

Algebra uses letters (like x and y) to represent numbers. An equation is a statement that two expressions are equal, for example:

Example (teacher): Solve 2x + 3 = 11.
  1. Subtract 3 from both sides: 2x = 8.
  2. Divide both sides by 2: x = 4.

Textbook note: Keep equations balanced. Use inverse operations (subtract to cancel add, divide to cancel multiply). Always check your solution by substituting it back.

Practice idea: Try solving 3x - 5 = 16 on paper. (Answer: x = 7.)

2. Graphs ‚Äî Points & Lines

A graph shows how two numbers relate on a coordinate plane. We write a point as (x, y), where x is horizontal and y is vertical.

Example (teacher): For the line y = 2x + 1, find two points:
  • If x = 0, then y = 1 ‚Üí point (0,1).
  • If x = 1, then y = 3 ‚Üí point (1,3).
Connect many points and you see a straight line.

Textbook note: Slope shows how steep a line is. For y = mx + c, m is the slope and c is the y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis).

Practice idea: Draw the line y = -x + 2 on graph paper. What are two points on it? (Answers: (0,2), (1,1).)

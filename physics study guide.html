üåç 1. Force

A force is a push or a pull on an object. Forces can make objects move, stop, or change direction.

Types of force include:

  • Gravity: Pulls objects towards the Earth. Example: Dropping a ball.
  • Friction: Resists motion between surfaces. Example: Brakes on a bicycle.
  • Push: Applying force away from yourself. Example: Pushing a chair.
  • Pull: Applying force towards yourself. Example: Pulling a rope in tug-of-war.

Practice: Identify forces in your surroundings. What forces are acting on a moving car? On a book on the table?

‚öôÔ∏è 2. Motion

Motion is the change in an object's position over time. To describe motion, we use:

  • Speed: How fast an object moves. Formula: Speed = Distance √∑ Time
  • Velocity: Speed with direction. Example: 10 m/s north.
  • Acceleration: Change in velocity over time. Example: Car speeding up on a road.

Practice: A car travels 100 meters in 20 seconds. What is its speed? Solution: Speed = 100 √∑ 20 = 5 m/s

üîÜ 3. Energy

Energy is the ability to do work. There are many forms:

  • Kinetic Energy: Energy of motion. Example: A rolling ball.
  • Potential Energy: Stored energy. Example: Water behind a dam or a stretched spring.
  • Heat Energy: Energy from temperature. Example: Sun warming the earth.
  • Light Energy: Energy from light. Example: Sunlight and lamps.

Practice: A book is on a shelf 2 meters high. What type of energy does it have? Answer: Potential energy.

üí° 4. Light

Light travels in straight lines and can reflect, refract, or split into colors.

  • Reflection: Bouncing of light. Example: Mirror.
  • Refraction: Bending of light. Example: Pencil in water looks bent.
  • Dispersion: Splitting of light into colors. Example: Rainbow through a prism.

Practice: Shine light through a glass of water. Observe bending or splitting. What is happening?

üîã 5. Simple Machines

Simple machines make work easier. They include levers, pulleys, inclined planes, screws, wedges, and wheels & axles.

  • Lever: A rigid bar used to lift loads. Example: Seesaw.
  • Pulley: Wheel with a rope to lift things. Example: Flag pole pulley.
  • Inclined Plane: Ramp to move objects up or down. Example: Loading ramp.

Practice: Identify simple machines in your home. Can you spot a lever, pulley, or ramp?
